# Observed Emotion Pattern: Nervousness



## What this means

Certain vocal cues, such as pitch variation or tension, may suggest nervousness. This is very common during presentations or evaluations.



## Why it happens

- Performance pressure

- Fear of judgment

- Lack of familiarity with the audience or setting



## What you can try

- Take a few slow breaths before speaking

- Start with a well-practiced opening sentence

- Focus on delivering one idea at a time



## Helpful reminder

Feeling nervous does not mean your speech is ineffective. Many confident speakers experience the same sensations.



## Important note

Emotion detection reflects patterns in speech signals and does not confirm how you feel internally.
